India’s IT Rules 2021 outline requirements for online platforms operating in India.

Key Requirements:
- Platforms must remove prohibited content after receiving government or court notices.
- Prohibited content includes:
  - Content threatening national security or public order
  - Harmful misinformation affecting health or public safety
  - Defamatory content
  - Obscene material, including sexual content involving children
  - Content encouraging violence or promoting enmity between groups

User Safety Obligations:
- Mechanisms for user complaints and grievance redressal must be available.
- Platforms must remove reported content within specific deadlines.

Disclosure Rules:
- Sponsored, paid, or promotional content must be clearly labeled.
- Platforms must maintain transparency around content moderation decisions.

Special Rules for Significant Social Media Intermediaries:
- Mandatory compliance officers
- User verification mechanisms
- Ability to trace “first originator” of messages (for legal cases)

Penalties include:
- Loss of safe harbor immunity
- Legal liability for user-generated content
